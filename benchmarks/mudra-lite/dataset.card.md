# MUDRA-Lite — synthetic loan-approval dataset card

## Summary

MUDRA-Lite is a deterministic, seed-controlled synthetic dataset generated by
[`generate.py`](generate.py) to exercise NyayaAI's fairness engine under a
biased-by-design scenario that mirrors documented India-context patterns.

- **~10,000 rows** (configurable with `--n`).
- **Seed 42** is the canonical demo seed.
- **Biased by design**: SC/ST/NT_DNT applicants are approved ~40 percentage
  points less often than GENERAL at the same income, tripping the
  disparate-impact 4/5ths rule.
- **Demo target**: pre-remediation demographic-parity ratio on `caste` lands
  in the ~0.55–0.65 band (matches the IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN §15 headline of 0.61).

## Provenance

Generated from **public-aggregate priors only**. No individual micro-data is
used or redistributed.

- **State shares & rural fractions** — Census of India 2011 aggregates,
  state-level only.
- **SC/ST shares** — Census 2011 national figures.
- **OBC share** — NSSO round aggregates (survey-based estimate).
- **Religion shares** — Census 2011 aggregates.
- **Mother-tongue dominant-language-by-state** — Census of India Language
  Atlas.

## Intended uses

- Deterministic regression test for NyayaAI's fairness engine.
- Demo fixture for the 96-second GSC video (the headline 0.61 → 0.94 metric
  shift runs on this dataset).
- Teaching fixture for fairness workshops — the bias structure is documented
  and intentional.

## Not intended for

- Training production credit-scoring models.
- Sociological inference about any real caste, religion or linguistic group.
- Comparing absolute approval rates to real MUDRA scheme outcomes.

## Known limitations

- State × caste priors are **coarse**: we do not tilt caste shares by state
  rurality beyond uniform national priors.
- OBC share is a point estimate; NSSO itself reports wide CIs.
- `surname_proxy_score` is synthetic (not derived from a real surname list);
  it exists to let the SPLS metric demonstrably fire on this dataset.
- `typing_cadence_quartile` is derived purely from urbanity + education; in
  reality it would come from device telemetry.

## Ethical notes

The caste penalty is **intentionally exaggerated** to make the bias visible
at small sample sizes. Do not read the magnitude as empirical; read the
*structure* (caste is a protected attribute; the 4/5ths rule applies; models
must be audited).

## Reproducibility

```
python generate.py --out data/mudra-lite.parquet --n 10000 --seed 42
```

Any change to this file, `generate.py`, or the pinned numpy/pandas versions
invalidates the golden reports checked into `benchmarks/mudra-lite/`.
